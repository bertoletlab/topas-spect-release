OpenTOPAS-SPECT

SPECT imaging simulation for OpenTOPAS.

A collimator, an energy-resolved detector, patient-like activity phantoms, moving-detector acquisitions, and the variance reduction that makes them tractable, built entirely from OpenTOPAS parameter files, with no C++ and no changes to the OpenTOPAS core.

What it adds to OpenTOPAS

OpenTOPAS is a powerful, well-validated Monte Carlo platform, but it ships without the geometry and scoring a clinical SPECT system needs. OpenTOPAS-SPECT adds those pieces as self-contained OpenTOPAS classes.

◉Collimators

Parallel-hole (round/hex/square, converging fan and cone-beam), pinhole and multi-pinhole, and slit-slat, plus CAD import for novel geometries.

▣Detectors

Energy-resolved NaI and CZT crystals, a general pixelated detector, and a StarGuide-style CZT ring, with pulse-height response and photopeak windowing.

◎Patient phantoms

Composable analytic phantoms with literature-grounded uptake for ¹⁷⁷Lu, ²²⁵Ac, ⁹⁰Y, and ¹³¹I therapies, or bring your own XCAT phantom.

↻Dynamic acquisitions

Prescribe a study in clinical console terms, projections, time per projection, heads, matrix, energy window, with native isotope decay on one clock.

⌖CT-driven motion

Derive collision-safe, contour-hugging detector motion from a patient CT, validated on a real Y-90 patient contour.

⚡Variance reduction

Forced-detection projection replaces the 1-in-5000 collimator lottery with an analytic projection, so a patient acquisition runs in minutes per view.

Validated, not just runnable

Every feature is checked against ground truth: absolute system sensitivity within 2% of the measured Symbia value, distance-dependent resolution, crystal energy response, and an end-to-end control experiment (a point source's sinogram traces the expected sinusoid to sub-pixel accuracy). A software test suite guards the tools and every shipped example.
